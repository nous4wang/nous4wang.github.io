I am a Senior Engineer at the **CMA Earth System Modeling and Prediction Centre (CEMC)**, China Meteorological Administration, working in the Satellite Data Assimilation Division. My research focuses on **satellite data assimilation**, **deep learning for remote sensing**, and their applications to **numerical weather prediction (NWP)**.

Research Interests
======
My work spans the intersection of satellite meteorology, atmospheric remote sensing, and artificial intelligence:

- **Satellite Data Assimilation** — Assimilating observations from hyperspectral infrared sounders (FY-4A/GIIRS, FY-3D/HIRAS), lidar, lightning imagers, and wind/precipitation retrievals into NWP systems.
- **Deep Learning for Remote Sensing** — Developing novel neural network architectures (Mamba, Transformer, U-Net variants) for cloud detection, 3D cloud structure retrieval, cloud-top height estimation, and precipitation nowcasting from geostationary satellite imagery.
- **Remote Sensing Algorithms** — Designing algorithms for cloud property retrieval from geostationary meteorological satellites (FY-series).

Background
======
I received my Ph.D. in Atmospheric Remote Sensing (2015), M.S. in Geoinformatics (2010), and B.S. in Automation (2007), all from the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC). Prior to joining CEMC in 2021, I worked as a Researcher at the National Satellite Meteorological Center (CMA) and as Technical Staff at the Earth Observation System and Data Center of the China National Space Administration (CNSA). In 2024, I was a Visiting Scholar at the Faculty of Science and Technology, University of Macau, working with Professor Yicong Zhou on machine learning for remote sensing and NWP.

I serve as a WMO Expert and actively collaborate with researchers across meteorological agencies and universities. A full list of my publications can be found on the [Publications](/publications/) page, and my CV is available [here](/cv/).
